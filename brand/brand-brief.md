# Brand Brief — Brunda Beauty Parlour

## Essence
- **Mission:** Make every customer — bride or walk-in — feel ceremonially cared for.
- **Vision:** Be the neighbourhood parlour of choice in south-west Bengaluru for weddings and occasion beauty.
- **Values:** Warmth · Care · Craft · Rootedness (South Indian tradition)

## Voice
- **Personality:** Warm · Ceremonial · Feminine · Unpretentious
- **Do say:** "pamper", "ritual", "special day", "your bridal look", "one stop", "close to you", "family"
- **Don't say:** "luxury spa", "wellness retreat", "clinical", "world-class", "premium spa experience" — those cues belong to five-star hotel spas, not a Bengaluru parlour. We want warm-premium, not corporate-premium.
- **Reading level:** Simple, conversational. English-first with a small Kannada acknowledgment.

## Visual direction
- **Aesthetic keywords:** editorial · ceremonial · gold-on-black · lotus/mandala motifs · restrained bridal jewelry references · candle-lit warmth (not neon)
- **Mood references:**
  - The business card itself — champagne gold serif on matte black
  - South Indian bridal photography (kanjivaram silks, gold ornaments, jasmine)
  - Editorial beauty magazine covers with type-forward hero layouts
- **Motion feel:** Subtle · Gilded · Reveal on scroll · No bounce/spring. Think "curtain lifts", not "toy pops".

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Generic beauty-salon stock imagery (blonde model, white subway tile, pink neon "beauty")
- Bootstrap-y card grids with harsh shadows
- Cold blue/teal accents (violates warm bridal palette)
- Loud pop-ups, chat bubbles, "book now!" spam
- Over-animated hero — no 3D blobs, no full-screen video autoplay
